How impact organizations scale, what endgames exist, and who ultimately pays at maturity.

üß© The 6 Endgames for Scaling Impact

Once an organization breaks out of early scaling, it can head toward six different endgames. Each path changes who pays and how you design the model.

1. Sustained Service

Org keeps running and growing with the biggest long-term budget. Example: BRAC.

Payor: Donors + user fees.

2. Commercial Adoption

Private sector takes the model to scale. Example: M-Pesa.

Payor: Market / commercial customers.

3. Government Adoption

The model is absorbed into public services and policy.

Payor: Taxpayers.

4. Replication

Others copy the model independently across regions or orgs.

Payor: Distributed funders.

5. Open Source

Blueprint is given away; ongoing cost is minimal.

Payor: Minimal / none.

6. Mission Achievement

Problem is solved and the org winds down ‚Äî rarest outcome.

Payor: Nobody.

üìà Growth Curve to Endgames

Start-up ‚Üí Proof of Concept ‚Üí Early Scaling ‚Üí Breakout Scaling ‚Üí Endgames diverge.

Early stages test product-market fit and evidence. Breakout scaling is the last shared phase; after that, the path depends on which endgame is realistic given the payor.

üè¶ The Ultimate Payor

The Ultimate Payor asks who bears the cost at scale when grants disappear. In the long run there are only three possibilities.

User / Customer
Direct fees, subscriptions
Government / Taxpayer
Public budgets, reimbursements
Market / Commercial
Private sector adoption
‚úÖ The Simple Test

"If all grants disappeared tomorrow, who would keep paying?"

This question surfaces the real endgame ‚Äî and whether the model can survive beyond philanthropy.

üîÅ Pay for Success / Social Impact Bonds

Pay-for-success structures create a circular accountability loop: funders take upfront risk, service providers deliver, evaluators verify, and government pays only for outcomes.

1 Investors fund the program upfront.
2 Service providers deliver interventions.
3 Independent evaluators measure outcomes.
4 Government repays investors only if targets are met.
